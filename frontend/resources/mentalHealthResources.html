If you are experiencing an emergency or require immediate assistance, please call or text the Mental Health Crisis Line 9-8-8 or reach out to a 24/7/365 days per year crisis counselor available through ODU by calling 757-683-4401, option 2.

If you feel that someone or yourself is in immenent danger, please call 9-1-1 or ODU's Police Department 757-683-4000.

Please visit ODU's resource page on emergencies by accessing the link provided in the navigation bar on the left as well to gain more insight on what assistance may be best or to learn more about how to help a fellow student.

ODU Mental Health Resources

Old Dominion University Office of Counseling Services

Explore ODU's homepage for the Office of Counseling Services to see what they have to offer or schedule an appointment with one of ODU's counselors.

ULifeline

Visit ULifeline, a resource free to all ODU students that includes information on handling anxiety, stress, and depression. ULifeline includes free mental health screeners as well as additional resources for crisis and support services.

Old Dominion University Health Promotion Office

ODU's Health Promotion Office uses and shares evidence-based practices and resources to help students with various wellness topics. The Health Promotion Office also provides information on joining a Peer Support Group or signing up for a meeting with the Peer Mental Health Ally Program, which connects students with other students in a safe place to discuss various topics.

Old Dominion University Student-Athlete Mental Health and Well-Being Center

The ODU Student-Athlete Mental Health and Well-Being Center (SAMWC) aims to assist Division I athletes at ODU through collaborative care, psychoeducation, and group and individualized therapy to athlete students at ODU. All services are free of charge for ODU athletes.
